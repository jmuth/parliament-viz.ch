BLA BLA BLA MOTIVATION
The motivation for this project comes from the gulf existing between the political world and citizens. Even though all the information is freely accessible on the official website of the Swiss Parliament, it amounts to a substantial number of papers and is hard to understand.
The Freedom of Information Act came into force on 1 July 2006. It is intended to promote transparency with regard to the purpose, organisation and activities of the federal administration, while guaranteeing access to official documents produced after 1 July 2006.

Explanation

A small explanation is given here about the different functionalities of this visualization. If you have trouble understanding it, it may be useful to read these few lines.

This visualization is focused on the councilors and their interactions inside the parliament. The base visualization shows where the councilors are sitting in the parliament. Each circle represent one of the councilor.

Moving the mouse over a circle

If you move your mouse over one of the circle, you will get information about this councilor. You will also see that some points become transparent. This is used to show the relations between one councilor and all of the others. If the other circles are opaque, this means that the two councilors have a strong relationship. On the other hand, a transparent circle means that the two councilors do not have a strong relationship. On the right panel, you can choose between two type of relations:
Co-signed motions
The cosigners are the councilors who signed the motion proposed by the selected councilor.
Interventions
The speakers are the councilors who spoke with the selected councilor in the same debate.
The list of relations will also be shown sorted by the relationship between the selected councilor and all the other councilors.
You can also click on a councilor to select him/her. Then you can move your mouser over the other councilors to get a few information on the right (under the options). If you want to deselect a councilor, you have to click on him/her again.

Right panel

The right panel will provide you some information about the councilor. At the top, some basic information are given. Then the timeline of their intervention is provided. If you move your mouse over a bar, you will get a bit more information.
